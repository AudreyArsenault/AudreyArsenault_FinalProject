Exploring population data and hate crimes
Vancouver, Canada

Research question

Has the number of police-reported hate crimes increased in the first year of the COVID-19 pandemic in Canada? What about Vancouver?

Background

Covid-19 is believed to have emerged from Wuhan, China back in December 2019 before spreading mondially (Gover, 2020). As Covid spread around the world, Asian-looking people reported an increase in racially motivated attacks from harassment to physical violence (Gover, 2020). Asian businesses were also the victime of attacts on their property, especially in chinatown areas. Thus, in addition to systemic racism, asian communities had to deal with further racism and xenophobia, enhancing hate crimes.

Hate crimes incidents increased by 47 percent in Canada from 1951 in 2019 to 2669 in 2020 (Statistics Canada, 2022). The main type of motivation was indeed race or ethnicity, which rose by 55 percent from 884 in 2019 to 1594 in 2020 (Statistics Canada, 2022). The two other most popular motivations are religion and sexual orientation, though both categories remained relatively stable before and after the beginning of the pandemic.

Although Canada is being promoted as a multicultural and diverse country, the city of Vancouver has gained the title of 'anti-Asian hate crime capital of North America' because Anti-Asian hate crime incidents increasing by 717 percent from 2019 (12) to 2020 (98) (Person, 2021)(The Guardian, 2021)(Vancouver Police Department, 2021).

Methodology

Hate crime definition: A hate crime is a criminal offence against a person or property that is based upon the victim’s race, national or ethnic origin, language, colour, religion, sex, age, mental or physical disability, sexual orientation, or gender identity or expression, or any other similar factor (Vancouver Police Department, 2022).

Study area: Vancouver

Vancouver is a coastal city located in the lower mainland region of British Columbia. It has a land area of 114.97 square kilometres (City of Vancouver, 2022). Vancouver has a population of 631,486 (according to the 2016 census). Vancouver's population is truly diverse with 52 percent of the population speaking a first language (or mother tongue) other than English. Approximately half of Vancouver's population is a visible minority (319,010 people in total), including South Asian (37,130), Southeast Asian (17,120) Chinese (167,180), Japanese (10,315), Filipino (36,460), black (6,345), Latin American (10,935), etc. (Canadian Census, 2016).

Data collection

After acquiring police-reported hate crime data in Canada from 2016 to 2020, I cleaned up the excel sheets and changed the order from the largest to the smallest. Then I proceeded to extract the data for the top 10 cities for each year, highlighting Toronto, Montreal, and Vancouver always in the top 3. I was interested in learning about the type of motivation for hate crimes from race or ethnicity to religion, sexual orientation, languages, disability, sex, etc. Through Jupyter Notebook, I created bar graphs to illustrate the changes in hate crimes from 2016 to 2020, and a pie chart to illustrate the type of motivation. The data was not available for 2021 and 2022 which is why I only studied the first year of the pandemic (2020).

Results

The numbers speak for themselves: there was a surge in hate crime in the first year of the pandemic across the country and even more so in Vancouver. When looking into the type of motivation, most hate crime are direcly related to race or etchnity, which further highlight a racism problem.

Discussion

Canada has a systemic racism problem, which was present prior to Covid-19. This has a direct correlation with mental health. In the United States, there was an increased of 22 percent of people seeking Mental Health America, including an increased of 39 percent among Asian American (Gover, 2020). Indeed this problem needs to be addressed in order to keep people safe both physically and mentally. The results of this study are incredly worrisome, and further research should be done when the data for 2021 is available.
